Getting App Logs



Tips (for reference only):
  • Logs will help troubleshoot:
    • Arlo app cosmetic issues
    • Arlo app crash issues
    • Device offline issues
    • Geofencing
    • Image/video quality issues
    • Issues related to Modes
    • CVR
    • Streaming
    • Delay on Notifications
  • Logs are currently encrypted and cannot be decrypted by anyone else other than Engineering because of security issues.
    • Logs can be decoded only through the Arlo Proprietary tool.
  • Logs do not contain customer passwords or library contents.
    • Logs contain OS, model, phone version, serial number, user ID, firmware version, etc.
  • Log title contains details that make it easy for Support to see what version of the app is used and when problem happened:
    • App version (x.x.x)
    • App build (x)
    • Date (M/DD/YY) and time
  • Logs get overwritten (because of file size) in 2 hrs, 4 hrs, 1 day, based on the activity in the app.
    • It is best to ask the customer to reproduce the issue and immediately send logs to the support team.


  1. Log into the Arlo iOS app and triple tap the Arlo icon at the top of the screen.
  2. Tap Email logs in the pop-up displayed at the bottom of the screen.
  3. Have the customer email the logs to themself.
    Once the email is received, ask the customer to attach the logs to their support ticket.
