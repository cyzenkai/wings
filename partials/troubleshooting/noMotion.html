Motion Detection Troubleshooting Guidelines


  1. Verify the issue with the customer to ensure this is a motion detection problem.
    • Cases should be tagged under the motion detection code if cameras do not detect motion and record video.
    • If this is not a motion detection issue, tag the case according to the applicable symptoms.
  2. How many cameras are affected?
    • Acquire camera serial numbers.
    • Also ask if camera is part of the kit or an add-on camera.
  3. Were the affected cameras were working before?
    • If so, ask when the cameras stopped working.
  4. Verify the customer has Arlo Smart enabled.
  5. Check the following camera settings/status:
    • Device status (Offline/Online)
    • Camera On/Off under My Devices
    • Mode settings
    • Camera Rule settings
      1. Check if Rule settings are correct (set to detect motion and record video).
      2. Camera rules should not be set to DO NOTHING
    • Sensitivity levels
    • Recording duration
  6. Check if there were changes in:
    • Camera settings
    • Location/placement
    • Firmware
    • New devices/appliances added to the environment that could cause interference
  7. Always verify the following and indicate in case notes:
    • How high did the customer mount the camera above the ground?
    • What is the distance of the camera from the base station?
    • Are there any walls between the camera and base station?
      1. If yes, what is the building material?
    • What is the distance of the recorded person/object from the camera?
    • Are there any obstructions within the cameraâ€™s motion detection angle/range?
    • What is the camera position/placement? (cameras should not be pointed through glass)
    • Does manual recording work as expected?
    • Are you able to stream the camera?
  8. Try Motion Detection Test to see if the camera responds when triggered (LED: blinking amber).
  9. Get a screenshot of what camera is viewing (without a person in the screenshot).
  10. If the customer mentions that they have already troubleshot the problem themselves and they do not want to go through additional troubleshooting:
    • Ask what steps they have done so we can evaluate if there are missing steps and make additional recommendations.
    • Documents the steps in the case notes.
