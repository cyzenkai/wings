Video Doorbell

Can you briefly describe what Arlo Video Doorbell is and why it was created?

Designed to capture what was traditionally limited with competitive video doorbells, the new front entry solution allows users to get a bigger, more precise picture of their front porch. The Video Doorbell offers features such as a high-definition camera with an ultra-wide field-of-view along with clear, two-way audio for users to simultaneously see and speak to visitors.
What makes Arlo Video Doorbell superior to other video doorbells in the market?

Arlo Video Doorbell is not only compatible with all the products in Arlo’s ecosystem, but also with other smart home products such as the Amazon Alexa, Google Assistant, and IFTTT. Arlo Video Doorbell offers more comprehensive benefits with its easy DIY setup, quick reply messaging feature, 180-degree field-of-view,  night vision, and advanced software capabilities.
Do I need to purchase other Arlo devices in order to use the Video Doorbell?

No, Arlo Video Doorbell can operate as a standalone product, although it is compatible with other devices within Arlo’s ecosystem.
Does the Arlo Video Doorbell need a base station or does it work like an Arlo Q in which it connects directly over Wifi?

No, the Video Doorbell does not need a base station as it connects directly to your 2.4Ghz home WiFi network.
What video resolution does the Video Doorbell output?

It records and outputs video in HD 1536 x 1536 square resolution.
What are the minimum requirements to use Arlo Video Doorbell?

To install Arlo Video Doorbell, users must have a wired doorbell and chime with existing electrical wiring, voltage between 16V AC and 24V AC, and a 10VA transformer.
Why does the Arlo Video Doorbell have a wired design?

The Arlo Video Doorbell requires a connection to a wired mechanical or digital chime in order to enable continuous power, reduce latency, and capture and transmit videos in real-time so users won’t miss a moment.
How easy is it to install Arlo Video Doorbell?

Simply download the Arlo app and follow the setup instructions for Arlo Video Doorbell. Then connect the power kit to your existing chime and replace your existing doorbell with your Arlo Video Doorbell. A Phillips head screwdriver is required to install the doorbell (not provided). Users may also benefit from having a drill, a level, and a ladder to reach the chime if needed.
Can I install the Arlo Video Doorbell if I don’t have an existing doorbell?

Arlo Video Doorbell requires a connection to a wired mechanical or digital chime in order to enable continuous power, reduce latency, and capture and transmit videos in real-time so users won’t miss a moment.
What comes with the Arlo Video Doorbell?

The Arlo Video Doorbell is packaged with the doorbell and flat mounting plate (these parts are attached to each other), power kit, release pin, angled mounting plate, wall screws and anchors, optional wire extensions, wire fasteners and extra screws.
Just how wide is the viewing angle on Arlo Video Doorbell?

The lens is a 180-degree lens, with a matching wide range for motion detection. The new Video Doorbell users to see a person from head to toe or a package on the ground.
What is the distance of sight/line of sight of the Arlo Video Doorbell from the Base Station?

The Arlo Video Doorbell is designed to connect directly to your home 2.4GHz WiFi network -- no base station required! As for range, the Arlo Video Doorbell is designed to meet the same wireless range requirements as Arlo’s wire-free camera lineup, up to 300 feet, line-of-sight, for your WiFi router. This distance can vary depending on obstacles such as concrete or stucco walls, metallic barriers or WiFi interference.
What is the average internet connection speed required for the Arlo Video Doorbell?

A high-speed internet connection with at least 2Mpbs upload is recommended to sustain the average bandwidth use of the Arlo Video Doorbell.

The motion detection sensitivity can be adjusted on the Arlo Video Doorbell to best suit your needs and front entry environment. The best area for motion detection is 3 to 15 feet from the doorbell.

Can you tell me more about the Arlo Video Doorbell notifications?

Smartphone notifications are also included as part of the 3 months trial of Arlo Smart upon purchasing Arlo Video Doorbell. Arlo’s new rich notifications (currently available in the US only) enable users to take action immediately upon receiving a notification, such as activating a siren, calling a friend, or calling 911 (for U.S. only). Users can also use Silent Mode to disable push notifications and silence the chime.

Can the Arlo Video Doorbell pair with Arlo cameras? If so, which cameras, and how does this work?

The Arlo Video Doorbell cannot pair with a camera like the Audio Doorbell does. This is because Video Doorbell has its own camera. Cross-triggering is the same as existing wire-free cameras – motion/audio detection from the Video Doorbell can trigger other cameras to record.

What is the difference between VoIP and SIP?

VoIP (Voice Over Internet Protocol) is limited to voice transmissions only whereas SIP (Session Initiation Protocol) is a communications protocol that is widely used for managing multimedia communication sessions such as voice and video calls.

Does the Video Doorbell have full duplex audio?
