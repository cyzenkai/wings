Introduction - Direct Storage Access

What is this feature?
We are introducing a new free feature to the Arlo app, Direct Storage Access, that will allow users to browse the contents of a microSD card or USB drive attached to their Ultra or Pro 3 SmartHub.

Why are we releasing?

  • After we launched Ultra, which required an Arlo Smart subscription, users were upset that Arlo no longer offered free cloud storage.
  • Retail accounts were similarly upset and warned that they would not carry Ultra or Pro 3 if we did not offer some sort of free recording storage and access option.

Major Features

  • In-app access to video recordings stored on your SmartHub, with 30 days of history.
    To access recordings on SmartHub older than 30 days, user must physically remove storage device and plug into computer. (Assumes that automatic overwrite has also not deleted these recordings)
  • Download videos from your SmartHub directly to the Arlo app on your iOS or Android device.
  • Share videos with others, or save to your phone’s camera roll to keep permanently.
  • Can be used in addition to Arlo Smart – have the best of both worlds!
    For Ultra, resolution in SmartHub will match resolution in cloud (i.e. Arlo Smart Premier on Ultra = 2K on cloud and SmartHub); also dependent on selecting “Best Quality” in local storage settings. (If best quality is OFF, then local recording = 1080p)

Advantages - Direct Storage Access

  • Flexibility - Anywhere access to recordings stored on the SmartHub at home.
  • 100% Control - Local recordings are never stored on the Arlo Cloud.
  • Capacity - Swap out storage devices and keep all your recordings. Forever.
  • Cost - No subscription required.

Requirements - Direct Storage Access

Supported devices which supports this feature:

  • VMB5000 (Ultra Smarthub)
    • microSD card inserted into SmartHub, formatted to FAT32
    • Capacity >= 16GB
  • VMB4540 (Pro 3 Smarthub)
    • USB drive inserted into SmartHub
    • Flash drives are recommended
    • Note: Not all external hard drives will work; hard drive must be USB 2.0 compliant and formatted to FAT32.

    Older, thicker portable drives may not work as the SmartHub does not provide enough power; 500GB, 1TB, and 2TB slim portable drives released by Seagate and WD in the past two years should be compatible, as should all desktop (wall-powered) external hard drives.

    Maximum capacity possible is 2TB.

  • Arlo camera that connect to SmartHub (ie, Arlo Q, Baby, Go, and Video Doorbell are currently excluded)
  • Owner account login
  • iOS or Android app version 2.12 or higher



Enabling - Direct Storage Access

  1. Go to Smarthub settings
  2. Go to Storage Settings
  3. Enable "Record Locally
  4. Enable "Direct Storage Access"
  • Once enabled, the user will be able to access video recordings that are stored on the storage device (microSD or USB) connected to their SmartHub while on the same network as the SmartHub
  • For example – if on the same WiFi network that the SmartHub is on, the user will be able to access the clips stored on their SmartHub.
  • With no further setup, if the user turns off WiFi or leaves the network, they will not be able to access their clips

Enabling for Outside the Local Network - Direct Storage Access

Use a VPN

  • The router connected to the SmartHub must provide a VPN (Virtual Private Network).
  • On the user’s iOS or Android device, the device must be connected to this VPN.
  • Very few routers have this functionality
  • No additional steps in the Arlo app are required for this option

Set up Port Forwarding

  • The Arlo app can provide basic instructions to the user for setting up port forwarding
  • User must go into their router settings and define ports that can allow the SmartHub to be accessed from outside the home
  • Port Forwarding literally opens a whole in the user’s network; users concerned about Port Forwarding should use VPN or subscribe to Arlo Smart for access outside the home



Enabling for Port Forwarding - Direct Storage Access

  1. In Storage Settings, scroll down to Port Forwarding.
  2. User must accept the risk of using PortForwarding:
    Port Forwarding gives a direct connection from the Internet to your Arlo Smarthub.
    By using port forwarding, you risk exposing your Smarthub and the contents of any device connected to it to the outside world.
    Use port forwarding at your own risk.
  3. User sees the configuration required for their router.
    4. Note: User can refresh the ports (in case of conflict with another application, or for security). Ports are randomly assigned and cannot be specifically controlled by the user.
Note: We will NOT walk a user in how to set up port forwarding (or VPN) on their specific router.

We will only tell them how to get the Port Forwarding information in the Arlo app, and advise that they must contact their router manufacturer, user manual, and/or ISP for setting up Port Forwarding on their specific router.

Accessing Locally Stored Recordings: Option A - Direct Storage Access

  1. Go to Devices Screen.
  2. For cameras that do NOT have cloud recording (ala no Smart plan and no 7 Day Free Cloud), users can access their library (pre-filtered to that camera) via the new “Library badge” icon.

    Note: Because we don’t scan the SmartHub library until you open it, the Arlo app has no way of knowing how many SmartHub-only clips were created since the last viewing.

    So, we have this new icon to represent the ability to filter the library to that camera.



Accessing Locally Stored Recordings: Option B - Direct Storage Access

  1. Go to Library.
  2. Click on source selector, tap on SmartHub.
  3. Library shows locall stored clips.

    Note: Thumbnails are not shown as we generate thumbnails today via the cloud.

    However, when a clip is downloaded and on the device, that is when the user will see a thumbnail (as we can process a thumbnail after it is downloaded to device)



Playing Locally Stored Recordings - Direct Storage Access

  1. Tap on a clip.
  2. Clip will download to Arlo app and play.
  3. User can save or share clip.

    Note: Because clips do not get stored on the cloud, user cannot favorite or donate clips.

    Furthermore, for security, we do not allow clips on SmartHub to be deleted.



Deleting Files Stored Locally on App - Direct Storage Access

  1. Go to settings & Cache.
  2. Tap on "Delete downloaded videos."

    Note: “Deleting downloaded videos” will ONLY delete videos that have been downloaded to the Arlo app.

    It will NOT affect videos on the SmartHub itself.

    This feature exists in case the user needs to free up storage on their device.
