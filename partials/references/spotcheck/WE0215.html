Issue:

Some 2-Camera Arlo Pro2 Black systems, model VMS4231P-100NAS sold exclusively at Best Buy in were labeled as having Local Storage capability when they contain a VMB3500B Black Base Station that does not support Local Storage.

 

Symptoms:

  • Customer purchased 2-Camera Arlo Pro 2 kit from Best Buy (must be system VMS4231P-100NAS)

Solution:

L1:

  • Let the customer know we will ship them a Base Station capable of Local Storage
  • Log a case against the Base Station VMB3500BNA with below Disposition tagging

L2:

  • Create a NBD RMA (Shipping waived) for the customer.
  • Select PN VMB4540B-100NAB. If unavailable, select PN VMB5000B-.

Disposition

Case Sub-Type: Complaints

Problem: Packaging

Symptoms: Wrong info on box

Root cause: RMA Device

 

Please document to CEP with above Disposition Code above and attach to ISSUE-11160 – “VMS4231P Labeled as having Local Storage when not available “

 

LOW BATTERY MESSAGE ON Arlo VIDEO DOORBELL

 

Overview

Customers report receiving a low battery message sometime after onboarding the Video Doorbell. This message might be seen minutes, days or weeks after onboarding the Video Doorbell. It is possible that this message is a result of the customer’s transformer being on the borderline of having sufficient power to operate the Video Doorbell to its fullest potential.

 

Symptom

Customers report seeing a low battery message days, weeks or soon after they have onboarded their Video Doorbell.

 

L1 Action

  • Immediately pull system logs on contact
  • Attach system logs and screenshots to the case
  • Did the customer receive a Low Battery Warning?
  • How long after installing the device did the customer receive a low battery warning?

 

  • Gather the following information related to Video Doorbell low battery reports:

Transformer

    • Transformer rating (e.g. 16V AC 10VA)
    • Pictures of transformer and wiring
    • Age of transformer
    • Any other device using the same transformer (e.g. other doorbells – traditional or video)
    • Note approximate distance between the transformer and Video Doorbell

Chime

    • Make and model of chime
    • Picture of chime and wiring
    • Number of chimes connected to the same transformer
    • Estimated wire length connecting doorbell to the transformer (approximate distance between doorbell and transformer)
    • If user is willing, ask the customer to disconnect their chime for a while (a few days to a week) to see if the low battery alert goes away.
    • Confirm if the chime is not ringing with power kit attached

Doorbell

    • Ensure that the breaker is turned on and there is power going to the Video Doorbell
    • Double check the Video Doorbell AC connection wires on the back and make sure they are securely fastened
    • If user has a voltmeter, ask for the measured AC voltage across the two terminals on the back of the Video Doorbell
      • NOTE: If voltage meets minimum requirement, it may still be at risk of low-battery issue. In this case the customer’s transformer is possibly underpowered due to a variety of reasons: total load on transformer (e.g. more than one device working off transformer), longer than normal distance between transformer and video doorbell

 

L2 Action

  • Ensure system logs were pulled on contact. If not, pull them as soon as possible upon escalation to L2
  • Ensure system logs are attached to the case
  • Attach system logs, App logs and screenshots to CEP Issue 11016
  • Be sure to attach all related cases to Issue 11016
  • Gather the following information related to Video Doorbell low battery reports

Case Disposition

Case Sub-Type: Functionality

Problem: Video Doorbell functionality

Symptoms: Received low battery message

Root Causes:

  • Advised of transformer requirements
  • Advised to disconnect chime - for follow up

Case Subject: Customer received low battery message on Video Doorbell - Issue 11016

Case Description: Customer received low battery message on Video Doorbell - Issue 11016

 

Summary

As a recap, please ensure the following actions are taken:

  • Immediately pull system logs on initial contact
  • Attach system logs and screenshots to the case and CEP Issue 11016
  • Be sure to attach all related cases to Issue 11016
  • Gather the information related to Video Doorbell low battery reports as listed above for the transformer, chime and doorbell

 

UNUSUAL LOGIN ACTIVITY AND LOCKED ACCOUNTS

 

Overview

Arlo has experienced an issue where, during our routine health checks, we have identified what appears to be unusual login activity on several Arlo accounts. As a proactive measure, Arlo has locked the accounts of affected users, in case the login attempts were not by the authorized user. To regain access to their accounts, users will need to reset and change their passwords.

Symptom

Users will find their accounts are locked out, and will be unable to log in using their password. Users will also be notified via e-mail that their account has been locked.

L1 Action

  • Let the customer know that “Arlo has experienced an issue where during our routing health checks, we identified unusual login activity on several Arlo accounts. As a proactive measure, we have locked the affected accounts, in case the login attempts were not by the authorized user. To regain access to your account, you will need to reset the password.
  • Direct customers to my.arlo.com. Have the customer click the “Forgot Password?” link. Have users follow the password recovery process to reset their password
  • Recommend that the user use a unique password or passphrase that is not used for any other account.
  • Recommend that the user set up TwoFactor Authentication on their account. Refer the customer to https://kb.arlo.com/000062288/What-is-two-step-verification-and-how-do-I-set-it-up
  • Apologize for any inconvenience that this may have caused.
  • Attach the Case to ISSUE11164 - Unusual Login Activity and Locked Accounts

L2 Action

  • Be sure all cases are attached to ISSUE 11164
